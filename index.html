Welcome to a new way of presenting adaptive recreational sports with STEM related subjects. With the use of hardware, software and Paralympic sports; Bits To Sports can help bridge learning gaps and increase inclusion and bring more awareness of people with disabilities. This program is a work in progress. So far we are applying 2 sports and using well known educational robots and software to help illustrate the science of those sports.

Code.org teachers computer science concepts and shows the thought process of building a program through trial and error.

Geogebra explores the math and science behind sports, making them more relatable to students who are struggling in these subjects.

With the use of robotics such as finch robots, students with disabilities can demonstrate their logic process in maneuvering their assistive devices.

Boccia is a low impact and precision-based sport that could be played one-on-one or in teams.

Power wheelchair soccer is a fast pace and team-based sports.
